Learn Migration!

Reveal the truth about migration

Abstract

Debates on migration often appear in the news. They usually focus on a singular event or group of people and try to elicit emotional reactions from the reader. It is therefore hard to have an objective idea of the scale of the global phenomenon outside of this particular instance.

Our visualization offers this insight: you can see what is the migration flow from any country to any other averaged over 5 years. So the next time someone claims that there are too many immigrants from this particular country to that particular one, you have a mean to approve or disprove the claim. Besides, you can have an overview of what are the countries that have the highest (and lowest) numbers of immigrants overall and with respect to their total populations.

A recent such example is the European refugee crisis. We focused on this particular group of people migrating in the European Union from across the Mediterranean Sea or through Southeast Europe. While the media often presented this as a local one-off occurrence, the reality is way more constant and widespread. While this is a more specific example, it still is a big part of global migration.

Migration Flow
Let's start our "revealing the truth about migration" journey with a very familiar subject "Migration Flow"! As you all already know, "Migration Flows" refer to the number of migrants entering or leaving a given country during a given period of time, usually one calendar year. The flow map below has been built on the data which is aggregated with an interval of 5 years, hence when you select a year, think about it as the year that you selected plus the 5-ongoing years (i.e. selected year is 1990, the data shown on the map corresponds to 1990-1995). It is now time to discover the various functionalities of the migration map that we offer.

On the top right of the map, you can choose the map type as you wish. When you chose Migration Flows you will be allowed to play with the functionalities panel above the map where you can change the country, select a gender type, flow type, and data type whether it is normalised or not, and slide over the year. Which you can also discover below the map, there is a color bar for a selected map type based on the highest and lowest flow values or level degrees.

International Migrant Stock
Did you know that "Migrant Stock" and "Migration Flow" do not mean the same thing. Well, they are indeed different! "International Migrant Stock" is the number of people born in a country other than that in which they live. It also includes refugees.

To fulfil your curiosity about the international migrant stock numbers, we prepared you a guide. Choose a country from the dropdown list below and play with the year slider! You are welcomed to discover hover functionalities on our two-sided horizontal bar chart which gives you gender separated information. You will see the change over the 5-year period for the same age group when hovered on the age group of selected gender, and the number of international migrant stock, and how much percent of that age group is actually corresponds to that of selected gender and year. Last but not least, do not freak out when you see "No Previous Data", it is THE beginning of our data.

Refugees and Migrants Deaths
The Routes Taken by Refugees and Migrants and The Causes of Death Events

As from the recent Syrian Refugee crisis, we all know that the migration can be mortal. The survival ratio in fact depends on the route taken by the migrants or refugees. You want to learn which route ends up with more deaths and which routes are the more securer? Check our Sankey Diagram below by simply selecting a migration route, and an event year from the relevant dropdown list! You will be surprised of discovering where and by taking which route how many death/missing event happened for a selected year! Most importantly, do you have courage to figure out what was the reason for the event?
